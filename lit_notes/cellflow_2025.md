# Klein, Fleck, Theis et al. 2025 - CellFlow

**Venue:** bioRxiv 2025. doi:10.1101/2025.04.11.648220.

Full title: "CellFlow enables generative single-cell phenotype modeling with flow matching." Authors: Klein D, Fleck JS, Bobrovskiy D, Zimmermann L, Becker S, Palma A, Dony L, Tejada-Lapuerta A, Huguet G, Lin H-C, Azbukina N, Sanchis-Calleja F, Uscidda T, Szalata A, Gander M, Regev A, Treutlein B, Camp JG, Theis FJ. Posted 2025-04-17 (v1). License cc_by_nc_nd. Corresponding: Fabian J. Theis, Helmholtz Munich.

## Claim
CellFlow is a flow-matching framework that learns a conditional vector field mapping control single-cell distributions to perturbed distributions, where the condition encoder ingests structured experimental metadata (drugs, gene targets, cytokines, dosage, cell line, time) and embeds them with pretrained representations including ESM-2 for proteins and molecular fingerprints for small molecules. The pitch is that this combination handles complex, compositional perturbation specifications and supports prediction for perturbations unseen at training time, evaluated across cytokine screens, drug screens, gene knockouts, whole-embryo developmental perturbations, and organoid protocol screens.

## Method
- **Generative formulation.** Flow matching: control cells (source) and perturbed cells (target) live in a low-dim cell embedding (PCA or VAE latent). A time-dependent neural vector field v_theta(x_t, t, c) is regressed onto a predefined probability path between source and target conditional on c, with optimal transport coupling between source and target available via the OTFlowMatching solver. A second solver, GENOT, is also exposed for entropic OT-based flows. Inference samples by integrating an ODE (diffrax) from t=0 to t=1 starting at control cells.
- **Condition encoder.** Each experimental setup is decomposed into a heterogeneous set of covariates (e.g., one or several perturbagens, doses, cell type tokens, time, batch). Each covariate is mapped to its own embedding using pretrained representations where available (ESM-2 for protein products of perturbed genes or for protein ligands like cytokines; molecular fingerprints for small molecules; learned embeddings or one-hot for categorical covariates). The set of covariate embeddings is aggregated by attention-based pooling and DeepSets-style aggregation. Pooling implementations exposed in the released code are `SelfAttentionPooling`, `SeedAttentionPooling`, and `TokenAttentionPooling` (src/cellflow/networks/). The aggregated condition vector c is then injected into the velocity field; per the public docs the `ConditionalVelocityField` integrates conditions via concatenation with optional layer normalization and linear projection, with attention-based pooling on the condition tokens upstream. So the conditioning is a token-set encoder + attention pooling on the perturbation side, then concatenation into the MLP velocity field on the cell side, rather than FiLM or AdaLN.
- **Protein/perturbation embeddings.** ESM-2 embeddings are used for protein products of perturbed genes and for protein ligands (cytokines). Drugs are represented by molecular fingerprints. The paper explicitly identifies the choice of these pretrained embeddings as the lever that enables OOD prediction for unseen perturbations and identifies their quality as the dominant limitation for held-out perturbations (see caveats). Exact ESM-2 variant and dimension are not enumerated in the publicly available abstract/README; in the codebase the embedding dim is configurable and the user supplies precomputed embeddings via the data manager.
- **Architecture.** JAX/flax implementation. Velocity field is an MLP-based `ConditionalVelocityField` with configurable `hidden_dims`, `time_encoder_dims` (sinusoidal + MLP time embedding), and a separate `ConditionEncoder` whose width and pooling head are configurable. Training uses optax; ODE integration uses diffrax.
- **Loss.** Conditional flow matching regression of the predicted vector field onto the target velocity along a chosen probability path (linear / OT-coupled), with optional displacement-cost regularization through the OT coupling. Sampling: Euler/Heun integration of the learned ODE.

## Datasets / experiments
The paper applies CellFlow across cytokine stimulation screens (PBMC cytokines), drug screens (sci-Plex-style), genetic perturbation screens (Perturb-seq lineage including Replogle-like protocols), whole-embryo developmental perturbation atlases, and organoid protocol screens (in-house iNeurons-style data ship as tutorial datasets `cellflow.datasets.ineurons` and `cellflow.datasets.pbmc_cytokines`). Evaluations include OOD generalization to held-out perturbations (held-out drugs, held-out genes, held-out cytokine combinations) and to unseen combinations. Concrete N cells / N perturbations per dataset are reported in the bioRxiv full text figures (not pasted here verbatim because the html mirror returned 403; numbers should be pulled from the PDF before any quantitative claim is made downstream).

## Metrics
Distributional metrics on the perturbed cell embedding: energy distance (e-distance, primary), MMD, Wasserstein-2. Regression-style metrics on aggregated pseudobulk or top-DEG axes: Pearson r and R^2 on differential expression vectors. Independent benchmarks (e.g., Tahoe-100M analyses, PerturBench) report median log1p e-distance between predicted and true perturbed cells across Tahoe-100M, sci-Plex-3, and Replogle, with CellFlow as one of the compared methods.

## What we steal for ConfPert
CellFlow is a wrappable predictor in the ConfPert predictor suite. Per the ConfPert spec we explicitly drop "ESM-derived perturbation embeddings" as a contribution because the embedding contribution is owned by CellFlow. ConfPert's value-add is calibrated uncertainty: we wrap CellFlow's per-condition sample cloud with conformal calibration (e.g., split conformal on per-condition residual or e-distance scores, or distributional conformal in the embedding space) to deliver coverage-valid prediction sets / intervals over single-cell phenotypes, including under perturbation shift. We do not compete on flow-matching capability or on perturbation embeddings.

## Failure modes / caveats
- Flow-matching training is compute-heavy; OT coupling adds Sinkhorn cost per minibatch.
- ESM-2 embeddings inflate the condition encoder I/O dimensionality (650M-param ESM-2 yields 1280-dim per residue / per-protein pooled vector); embedding precomputation is mandatory.
- OOD generalization to truly unseen perturbations is bounded by how well ESM-2 / molecular fingerprints capture the *functional* axis of the perturbation, not just sequence/structure similarity. The CellFlow authors flag this directly: "the effectiveness of predicting responses to entirely unseen perturbations strongly depends on the quality of condition embeddings and their ability to capture functional properties, and while CellFlow leveraged existing embeddings like ESM2 for proteins and molecular fingerprints for small molecules, these representations may not optimally encode the functional roles relevant to cellular responses."
- Independent reports (PerturbDiff arXiv 2025; SP-FM arXiv 2026) note that vanilla CellFlow holds the source distribution fixed (Gaussian or PCA-of-controls) and conditions only the velocity field, which can underperform on highly heterogeneous perturbation responses where the base distribution itself should shift with condition. PerturbDiff and STATE both outperform CellFlow on subsets of Tahoe-100M and PBMC e-distance benchmarks per their reported tables. ConfPert should not assume CellFlow is uniformly the strongest base predictor and should treat predictor choice as per-dataset.
- Cell embedding dependence: CellFlow operates in a PCA or VAE latent, so all distributional metrics inherit that embedding's geometry; conformal coverage guarantees from ConfPert will be over the same embedding unless we explicitly decode and recalibrate in count space.
