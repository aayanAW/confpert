# Tahoe-100M 2025 (Vevo / Arc / Biohub)

**Venue:** bioRxiv preprint 2025.02.20.639398, posted 2025-02-24 (Tahoe Therapeutics, formerly Vevo Therapeutics; released as the inaugural contribution to Arc Institute's Virtual Cell Atlas, in partnership with the Chan Zuckerberg Biohub). Corresponding author Johnny Yu, Vevo Therapeutics. License on bioRxiv: CC-BY-NC. License on Hugging Face data release: CC0-1.0.

## Claim
Tahoe-100M is a 100,648,790-cell single-cell drug-perturbation atlas spanning 50 cancer cell lines and 1,100 small-molecule perturbations across three doses, generated on the Vevo Mosaic "cell village" platform. The authors frame it as the largest public single-cell perturbation dataset, "50x larger than all previously public drug-perturbed data combined," and release it as an open foundation-model training resource for virtual-cell modeling.

Verbatim from the abstract: "Here, we present Tahoe-100M, a giga-scale single-cell atlas of 100 million transcriptomic profiles measuring how each of 1,100 small-molecule perturbations impact cells across 50 cancer cell lines. Our high-throughput Mosaic platform, composed of a highly diverse and optimally balanced 'cell village', reduces batch effects and enables parallel profiling of thousands of conditions at single-cell resolution at an unprecedented scale."

## Method

**Cell village (Mosaic).** A panel of 50 established cancer cell lines were pooled in equal ratios into a single Mosaic well, so every drug perturbation is delivered to all 50 lines simultaneously. Demultiplexing back to cell-line identity is done from genotype (cell-line metadata schema includes `Cell_ID_DepMap`, `Cell_ID_Cellosaur`, driver-gene fields). The pooling design is the central batch-effect mitigation: every drug well contains the same 50-line mixture, so cell line is balanced across all conditions.

**Compounds and doses.** 1,100 small-molecule perturbations (the press materials and one Parse blog round to "1,200"; the manuscript and HF dataset card consistently say 1,100). Each compound was assayed at three concentrations: 0.05 µM, 0.5 µM, and 5 µM. Plate layout: fourteen 96-well plates, each plate carrying ~94 unique drugs plus two DMSO control wells (DMSO_TF marks vehicle controls in the schema). Cytokine perturbations (~100) are also referenced in some accompanying materials.

**Treatment and fixation.** "Following 24-hour treatment, cells were dissociated and fixed using the Evercode Cell Fixation v3 High-Throughput Plate-Based Workflow." Single-cell capture used the Parse Biosciences GigaLab combinatorial-barcoding pipeline: "Lysates were processed through Sections 2 and 3 of the Parse Biosciences GigaLab protocol in batches of 96 with the Hamilton Vantage liquid handling robot."

**Sequencing.** 1,786 sublibraries were converted to Ultima Genomics-compatible libraries and sequenced on the UG-100 sequencer at a mean read depth of approximately 10,000 reads per cell.

**QC.** Standard scRNA filters applied: removal of low-quality cells, doublets, and high-mitochondrial cells. Sample metadata exposes per-sample QC summaries (`mean_gene_count`, `mean_tscp_count`, `mean_mread_count`, `mean_pcnt_mito`). DNAnexus walkthrough notes that three low-quality cell lines were dropped downstream, leaving 47 lines for the most-filtered analyses, while the headline 50-line count refers to the as-released dataset.

**Public release format.** Hugging Face dataset `tahoebio/Tahoe-100M` ships in Parquet (primary) and h5ad (Arc Virtual Cell Atlas mirror); also accessible via Hugging Face `datasets` (Arrow-backed) and via DNAnexus public mirror (AnnData and Parquet). Schema (verbatim fields): `genes` (sequence<int64> token IDs), `expressions` (sequence<float32> raw counts), `drug`, `sample`, `BARCODE_SUB_LIB_ID`, `cell_line_id`, `moa-fine`, `canonical_smiles`, `pubchem_cid`, `plate` (1-14). Side tables: gene metadata (`gene_symbol`, `ensembl_id`, `token_id`), sample metadata (with QC summaries and `drugname_drugconc`), drug metadata (`targets`, `moa-broad`, `moa-fine`, `human-approved`, `clinical-trials`, `gpt-notes-approval`, `canonical_smiles`, `pubchem_cid`), and cell-line metadata (DepMap and Cellosaurus IDs, organ, driver gene, driver variant zygosity/type/effect/mechanism).

## Datasets / experiments

- Cells (post-QC, as released): 100,648,790.
- Cell lines: 50 (47 after stricter QC).
- Drugs: 1,100 small molecules.
- Doses: 3 concentrations per drug (0.05, 0.5, 5 µM).
- Time point: single 24 h endpoint.
- Experimental conditions (drug x dose x cell line cells, after pooling): 56,829 unique conditions.
- Drug x cell-line pairs: ~60,000 (press materials).
- Plates: 14 x 96-well; sublibraries: 1,786.
- Sequencing depth: ~10,000 reads/cell mean.
- Coverage: full Cartesian product of 1,100 drugs x 3 doses x 50 cell lines is intended, with DMSO controls on every plate as anchored references.

## Metrics

The preprint reports (i) per-cell QC distributions (gene count, transcript count, mapped-read count, % mitochondrial) at the sample level; (ii) batch-effect mitigation evidence from the cell-village design (drugs are confounded with neither plate identity nor cell line because every plate hosts the same 50-line pool and DMSO is replicated within plate); (iii) reproducibility of dose-response signatures across the three concentrations; (iv) recovery of expected mechanism-of-action structure using the curated `moa-fine` and `moa-broad` annotations and `targets` field; (v) demonstrations that AI models trained on Tahoe-100M learn context-dependent gene function across the 50 cell-line contexts. Companion work Tahoe-x1 (Zhang et al., bioRxiv 2025.10.23.683759) trains a 3B-parameter perturbation foundation model on Tahoe-100M and reports downstream prediction benchmarks.

## What we steal for ConfPert

Tahoe-100M is dataset #5 for ConfPert. Concretely:

- **Cross-cell-line generalization at scale.** 50 cancer cell lines x 1,100 drugs x 3 doses is the only public dataset large enough to study how predictive uncertainty for a drug response transports across cell-line context. ConfPert calibration sets can be built per cell-line or per MOA cluster, and held-out cell lines provide a clean covariate-shift evaluation regime.
- **Calibration set + held-out test design.** Use per-plate DMSO wells as anchor controls, hold out a stratified subset of (drug, cell-line) pairs as the test fold, calibrate conformal scores on a disjoint (drug, cell-line) fold from the same plates to control for batch.
- **K3 (PRISM downstream drug-resistance) leverage.** Train ConfPert response predictors on Tahoe-100M cell-line responses, transfer to PRISM cell lines via cell-line embedding alignment (DepMap IDs are first-class in both), and use the conformal layer to flag PRISM lines that fall outside Tahoe-100M's coverage with non-trivial coverage gaps.
- **Dose-response heterogeneity as a shift axis.** Three doses per drug let us probe whether conformal sets remain calibrated under dose extrapolation (e.g., calibrate at 0.5 µM, test at 5 µM).
- **MOA-stratified conformal calibration.** The `moa-broad` / `moa-fine` annotations give a natural grouping for group-conditional conformal coverage.

## What we wrap

Dataset, not model. Public release on Hugging Face under CC0-1.0; Arc Virtual Cell Atlas mirror; DNAnexus mirror. We pull the Parquet/Arrow shards, canonicalize cell-line IDs through DepMap, and treat Tahoe-100M as a fixed training+calibration corpus. No retraining of the data-generating pipeline; downstream models (e.g., for K3) are ours.

## Failure modes / caveats

- **Single time point (24 h).** No kinetics; transient versus steady-state responses are conflated. Dose-response heterogeneity at 24 h may not reflect IC50 dynamics on slower drugs.
- **Cancer cell lines only.** 50 immortalized lines, all cancer. Generalization to primary cells, organoids, in vivo, or non-cancer contexts is unsupported by the dataset alone. The authors note the resource is "fundamentally designed as a broadly applicable perturbation atlas" but the empirical coverage is cancer.
- **Cell-village confounding within line.** Pooling reduces plate-level batch effects but introduces within-pool ecological effects: a drug that kills line A may shift the relative abundance and conditioned-medium composition for line B in the same well. This is a real scientific caveat for inferring monoculture drug response.
- **Single Mosaic platform vendor stack.** Parse GigaLab + Ultima UG-100 is a specific chemistry+sequencer combination; cross-platform reproducibility is not separately benchmarked in this preprint.
- **Read depth ~10k/cell.** Adequate for differential expression at the population level, shallow for rare-transcript or isoform-level analyses.
- **License split.** The bioRxiv text is CC-BY-NC (no commercial reuse of manuscript text); the Hugging Face data release is CC0-1.0. Confirm per-mirror license before redistribution.
- **Three low-quality cell lines dropped** in the strict-filtering pipeline (47 lines remaining). Document which line subset is used for each ConfPert experiment.
- **QC heterogeneity across cell lines.** Per-sample mean gene count, mean transcript count, and mean % mito vary by line; uncorrected, this leaks line identity into expression magnitude.
- **Drug count rounding.** Manuscript and HF card say 1,100 small molecules; press releases and X posts say "1,200." Use 1,100 as the load-bearing number.
