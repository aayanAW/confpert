# Corsello et al. 2020 - PRISM Drug Repurposing Resource

**Venue:** Nature Cancer 2020, 1(2):235-248. DOI 10.1038/s43018-019-0018-6. PMID 32613204. PMCID PMC7328899.

## Claim
Corsello 2020 reports a systematic viability profiling resource built on PRISM, a barcode-based pooled cell-line assay, with 4,518 drugs tested across 578 human cancer cell lines spanning 24 tumor types, generating roughly 2.6 million drug-cell-line viability measurements. The headline finding is that an unexpectedly large fraction of non-oncology drugs selectively kill subsets of cancer cell lines, and that the pattern of cell-line-level sensitivity is often predictable from the cell lines' molecular features (mRNA expression, copy number, mutation). The paper frames its main scientific axis as cell-line-level heterogeneity of response: which lines die, which lines resist, and what genomic features separate the two populations. Selective compounds are operationalized via a bimodality coefficient over the per-cell-line log-fold-change profile, so the dataset is explicitly indexed by within-population (cross-cell-line) bimodality.

## Method
PRISM is a pooled barcoded viability assay. Each of the 578 adherent cancer cell lines carries a unique 24-nucleotide DNA barcode stably integrated by lentivirus. Lines are pooled in groups of 25, plated, and treated with a chemical perturbagen. After 5 days the pool is lysed and the relative abundance of barcode mRNAs is read out by Luminex bead hybridization, giving a per-line growth/death readout for the entire pool in one well. The campaign has two stages: (1) a primary screen of all 4,518 compounds at a single 2.5 microMolar dose against 578 (or 562) cell lines, with SSMD-based plate filtering (cell line-assay plate combinations with SSMD < 2 are filtered out), and (2) a secondary screen of 1,448 active compounds at an 8-point, 4-fold dilution dose-response from 10 microMolar down to 600 picoMolar against 499 cell lines, in triplicate. Dose-response curves are fitted with the R package drc with the upper asymptote fixed at 1; IC50 is defined as the concentration c at which V(c) = 0.5; AUC is computed over the shared dose range. Batch effects across detection plates are removed with ComBat. Selective killing is quantified by computing the bimodality coefficient on the median-collapsed log-fold-change PRISM profile per compound. Genomic predictors are fit per compound via random-forest regression of AUC against CCLE multi-omic features, with feature importance reported as a Pearson-correlation-based score.

## Datasets / experiments
4,518 unique compounds in primary, of which 77% are non-oncology drugs by clinical indication. 578 adherent cell lines (also reported as 562 for some primary plates) spanning 24 tumor types. 1,448 compounds advanced to the secondary 8-point dose-response on 499 lines. Approximately 2.6 million primary drug-cell-line measurements (4,518 x 578) and roughly 1,448 x 499 x 8 doses x replicates in secondary. Concrete hits: 774 of 1,448 active compounds (53%) are originally non-oncology indications; 49 non-oncology compounds have selective and biomarker-predictable anti-cancer activity (Pearson predictor score > 0.4); 91 drugs kill at least 1% of lines at 625 nanoMolar or lower; only 0.8% of active non-oncology drugs show on-target killing concordant with CRISPR/RNAi knockout of the nominal target, vs 15.0% for active oncology drugs. Mechanistic case studies include disulfiram (resistance/sensitivity tracks 16q copy-number loss and MT1E/MT2A metallothionein expression), tepoxalin (sensitivity is positively driven by ABCB1 efflux-pump expression, an inversion of the usual ABCB1-confers-resistance pattern), vanadium-containing BMOV (Pearson -0.583 with SLC26A2 expression; SLC26A2 KO in OVISE confers resistance), and a PDE3A-SLFN12 cluster of 11 structurally diverse killers rescuable by PDE3A KO or trequinsin competition.

## Metrics
Per drug-cell-line: log MFI (log mean fluorescence intensity) collapsed over replicates, log-fold-change vs vehicle control, viability V(c), IC50, AUC over a fixed shared dose range. Per drug across cell lines: bimodality coefficient of the LFC profile (the operational definition of selectivity), random-forest AUC predictor with Pearson r reported as predictability, and feature importance over CCLE mRNA/CN/mutation features. Per assay plate: SSMD as the QC filter. Replicate-level standard error from vehicle control is used to characterize assay noise.

## What we steal for ConfPert
PRISM is the substrate for ConfPert K3 (calibrated subpopulation detection of drug-resistant cell-line sub-populations). Three properties make it the right testbed. First, the dataset is natively indexed by cross-cell-line heterogeneity: the bimodality coefficient over the LFC profile is the paper's own operational definition of a selective drug, which is exactly the bimodal predictive distribution structure ConfPert is built to extract via conformal sets over the predicted per-cell-line response distribution. Second, the secondary screen provides ground-truth dose-resolved viability per (drug, cell line) on 1,448 x 499 pairs, which gives both the labels for resistant subpopulation membership (high-AUC tail) and a continuous response for predictive calibration. Third, the paper reports CCLE-feature-driven random-forest predictors with Pearson r as the headline number; this is the uncalibrated baseline that ConfPert beats. The K3 evaluation runs as: train a perturbation predictor that emits a distribution over per-cell-line viability for a held-out drug; declare the resistant subpopulation as the upper mode (or upper conformal set); benchmark AUROC for separating PRISM ground-truth resistant lines (AUC > tau) from sensitive lines, against (a) point-prediction baselines collapsed to mean and (b) the Corsello random-forest predictor. Because PRISM also reports cases where bimodal selective killing is unpredictable from genomics ("6 non-oncology compounds showed selective bimodal killing patterns, but their activity was found to be unpredictable"), this gives a stratified slice where calibrated prediction sets should widen and where ConfPert's coverage should hold even when point predictors fail.

## What we wrap
Dataset, not model. PRISM Repurposing primary and secondary releases hosted at depmap.org/repurposing and on Figshare, downloadable as cell line info CSV, pooling info CSV, replicate-level log-fold-change CSV, mean fluorescence intensity CSV, replicate-collapsed treatment info, and dose-response fits. Secondary release files range 3.6 MB to 252.1 MB. We do not wrap the random-forest predictor; we treat it as one of the uncalibrated baselines.

## Failure modes / caveats
Pooled-format variance: the paper concedes "the PRISM Repurposing dataset tends to be somewhat noisier, with a higher standard error estimated from vehicle control measurements," attributed to "a combination of a longer assay duration, a smaller number of cells assayed, and/or variation attributable to growing cells in pools." Paracrine pooling artifacts are flagged as a theoretical risk: "It is conceivable that the pooling of cell lines results in paracrine-mediated mechanisms that modulate drug sensitivity. In practice, however, we have yet to observe such cell-cell interactions or any consistent discordance between PRISM and one-by-one viability profiling." Bulk-viability-only readout: PRISM measures barcode abundance, which is a bulk per-cell-line viability, not a transcriptomic or single-cell-resolved response, so it cannot directly supervise within-cell-line subpopulation resistance; ConfPert K3 has to treat the 578-cell-line panel itself as the population over which subpopulations (sensitive vs resistant modes) are defined. Adherent-only panel: 578 lines are adherent, so non-adherent malignancies (most hematologic) are out of scope. Acquired resistance via target downregulation is acknowledged: "when anti-cancer drug targets are not themselves cancer dependencies, it is possible that strong selective pressure will result in down-regulation of the target protein, resulting in drug resistance." Off-target dominance among non-oncology hits (only 0.8% on-target) means biomarker associations are often through non-canonical mechanisms (efflux pumps, metal chelators, transporters), which limits transfer of biomarker logic across drug classes.

PRISM gives the labels (per-line AUC, IC50, LFC), the bimodality coefficient gives the paper-native definition of a selective compound that ConfPert generalizes to a calibrated predictive set, and the Corsello random-forest predictor is the uncalibrated baseline to beat.
