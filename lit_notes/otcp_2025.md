# Klein, Bethune, Ndiaye, Cuturi 2025 - OT-CP: Multivariate CP via Optimal Transport

**Venue:** arXiv preprint, 5 Feb 2025. arXiv:2502.03609 [stat.ML]. Authors affiliated with Apple. No conference acceptance listed in v1. Concurrent and related to Thurin, Nadjahi, Boyer 2025 (arXiv:2501.18991), which the authors explicitly acknowledge.

## Claim
OT-CP extends split conformal prediction to vector-valued nonconformity scores by ranking them through an optimal-transport map that pushes the score distribution onto a uniform measure on the unit ball. The norm of the transported score becomes a univariate conformity score, so standard one-dimensional CP machinery applies and produces finite-sample marginal coverage at level 1 minus alpha for joint multivariate prediction sets, without distributional assumptions and without parametric copula or Gaussian estimation. The construction works for any approximation T-hat of the Brenier map, including the entropic Sinkhorn map, and the coverage guarantee survives the approximation because the radius is calibrated empirically rather than relying on closeness of the pushforward to the true uniform.

## Method
- Univariate background: classical split-CP set is `R_alpha,n = {y : F_n(S(x_n+1, y)) in [a,b]}` with `U_{n+1}([a,b]) >= 1 - alpha`. Center-outward view uses `T = 2F - 1` mapping P to uniform on `[-1, 1]`.
- Kantorovich rank: replace univariate CDF by the optimal Brenier map `T = grad phi` pushing the score distribution P forward to the spherical uniform U on the unit ball in R^d. Rank of z is `||T(z)||`. Quantile region: `R_alpha = {z : ||T(z)|| <= 1 - alpha}`. Coverage `P(Z in R_alpha) = 1 - alpha` follows from `||T(Z)|| ~ Uniform(0,1)`.
- Discrete construction: build target grid `U_{n+1}` on the sphere with `n+1 = n_R * n_S + n_o` points: `n_S` directions uniform on the sphere, `n_R` regularly spaced radii in `{1/n_R, 2/n_R, ..., 1}`, and `n_o` copies of the origin. Solve the optimal assignment problem (Eq. 13) to get the discrete map `T_{n+1}`.
- Proposition 3.2 gives the smallest radius for coverage 1 - alpha as `r_{alpha, n+1} = j_alpha / n_R` where `j_alpha = ceil( ((n+1)(1-alpha) - n_o) / n_S )`. Resulting set is `{y : ||T-bar_{n+1} composed S(X_{n+1}, y)|| <= r_{alpha, n+1}}`.
- Two practical fixes for split-conformal use:
  1. **OT merging** (Sec. 3.2): define `S_{OT-CP}(x,y) = ||T*(S(x,y))||` and treat its norm as a univariate score; apply standard split-CP to these norms. Reduces to Mahalanobis under affine transport between Gaussians (Remark 3.4).
  2. **Empirical-radius calibration under approximation** (Sec. 3.3, Lemma 3.5, Prop. 3.6): given any approximation T-hat of the Brenier map, define `r-hat_alpha = inf{r : U-hat_{n+1}(B(0,r)) >= 1 - alpha}` where U-hat is the pushforward of P_{n+1} through T-hat. The set `{y : ||T-hat composed S(X_{n+1}, y)|| <= r-hat_alpha}` has finite-sample distribution-free coverage `>= 1 - alpha` even when T-hat is biased.
- Implementation (Sec. 3.4): T-hat is the entropic map of Pooladian and Niles-Weed (2021) computed by Sinkhorn on the n by m kernel matrix `K_{ij} = exp(-||z_i - u_j||^2 / epsilon)`, with `T_epsilon(z) = sum_j p_j(z) u_j` where `p_j(z) propto exp( -(||z - u_j||^2 - g_j*) / epsilon )`. Sphere discretized via stratified radii and Gaussian-mapped low-discrepancy directions (Basu 2016). Implemented in OTT-JAX (Cuturi et al., 2022). Training cost O(nm); per-test transport O(m).

## Datasets / experiments
Setup follows Dheur et al. 2025 benchmark of multivariate regression: 24 tabular datasets, n in `[10^3, 5 * 10^4]`, input dim p in `[1, 348]`, output dim d in `[2, 16]`. Reported datasets include ansur2, bio, births1, calcofi, edm, enb, house, taxi, jura, scpf, sf1, sf2, slump, households, air, atp1d, atp7d, rf1, rf2, wq, oes10, oes97, scm1d, scm20d. Underlying predictor is the multivariate quantile-function forecaster MQF2 (Kan et al. 2022); OT-CP only consumes the conditional-mean point estimator, while M-CP uses the full conditional quantiles. Experiments split low-d regime (d <= 6) from high-d regime (14 <= d <= 16). Hyperparameters tested: epsilon in `{0.001, 0.01, 0.1, 1.0}`, m in `{8192, 32768}`. Default reported: epsilon = 0.1, m = 2^15 = 32768, 10 random seeds.

## Metrics
Marginal coverage (MC) at target levels and mean region size (Size), the latter estimated by importance sampling using MQF2 as an invertible normalizing flow at test time. Wall-clock time also reported (Fig. 3, Fig. 7). Region-size is the central comparison metric, conditional on coverage being satisfied. No conditional-coverage metric is reported in v1.

## What we steal for ConfPert
OT-CP supplies a non-parametric, finite-sample-valid multivariate conformal head built on Brenier or Sinkhorn ranks. ConfPert can plug OT-CP in directly as one of its joint distributional heads when the per-population score is vector-valued (e.g., per-cluster log-fold-change vectors, multi-marker effect vectors, or stacked moment statistics). The empirical-radius calibration of Lemma 3.5 / Prop. 3.6 is the load-bearing piece: it lets ConfPert use a coarse, cheap entropic map and still inherit a `1 - alpha` coverage certificate on the perturbation-effect prediction region. We adopt OT-CP as the formal foundation for our per-population multivariate head, citing it for our energy-distance and Wasserstein-based scoring.

## What we wrap
Procedure-only. We import the calibration recipe (compute Sinkhorn map on calibration residuals, pushforward, take empirical 1 - alpha quantile of norms, threshold at test time) and the OTT-JAX entropic-map estimator. No model architecture or training-time component is borrowed.

## Failure modes / caveats
- Sinkhorn cost scales as O(nm) per calibration run; per-test cost O(m). Authors find m = 32768 needed for d up to 16, which is heavy.
- Curse of dimensionality in entropic Brenier estimation (Chewi et al. 2024): performance edge over Merge-CP and M-CP shrinks above d = 6 and is unclear in the d in `[14, 16]` block.
- epsilon must be tuned per dimension: too small triggers known instabilities of unregularized OT, too large collapses T_epsilon onto the sphere; epsilon = 0.1 is the reported sweet spot under OTT-JAX normalization.
- The transport map must be recomputed if the score distribution shifts (Remark 3.3). Naive recomputation per candidate y is intractable, hence the merging trick.
- HetPert-style energy-distance scoring is a special case only when source-to-target transport is affine (Gaussian to Gaussian or uniform to uniform), per Remark 3.4. Outside that regime, OT-CP delivers strictly more flexible regions.
- Sinkhorn debiasing did not help here, matching Pooladian et al. 2022.
- Coverage statement is marginal, not conditional on x. Conditional coverage on subgroups is not addressed.
